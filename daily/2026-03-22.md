# Sunday, March 22, 2026

## What Happened

No sleeping in — early morning at church for the forming session of the new parish council. I've been re-elected for a second term, finishing seventh out of 15 or 16 candidates. That felt good, honestly. I was proud of that result. After the first term, I'd considered not running again — some of the tasks aren't exactly thrilling. But at the end of the day, it's community work, it connects me to people in the village, and it's a positive duty. Not a chore. Something I actively chose.

We voted to co-opt the candidates who didn't get elected, which I think is the right call. After the session, I delivered birthday presents from the parish council to a community member turning 80 — one of those traditions I genuinely appreciate.

**The highlight: bike ride with Felix.** Picked up my biking clothes and we headed out around 12:30 — a test ride to a nearby lake, prepping for our May 1st weekend tour. He took his mountain bike, I took the gravel. The first stretch was classic puberty mode — barely a word, just "hm" and "ha" when I tried to talk. He loosened up later though. We made it to the lake in just over an hour, some proper steep hills in there. Had lunch, and then he challenged me: "Can we be home before 3?" We rolled in at 2:58. Fun.

One honest self-check: I kept speeding up and losing him. This isn't about proving I'm faster. If we do another prep ride before May, I need to hold myself back. It's his trip too — not a race.

Felt properly tired but great afterwards. Did two rounds in the sauna when we got home — perfect finish. The sauna effect plus exhaustion knocked me out on the sofa later while watching TV with the family. Tried to stay awake, mostly failed. No shame in that.

Evening was quiet — another episode of Yellowstone, daily crypto checks, journaling for Saturday. Made a conscious decision to go to bed at 11:20 instead of pushing past midnight. And here's the big one: I decided this ride is the kick-off of the spring season. The momentum is there. I want to carry it through the week — maybe two runs or a run and a bike ride. Just get into that moving rhythm again.
