# Tuesday, April 21, 2026

## What Happened

**Packed day. Two completely different worlds. Both delivered.**

## KNIME Level 3 — The Premiere

First ever KNIME Level 3 expert training at Siemens, delivered for SI division. Four hours, two short breaks. I'd never given this level before and I'd be lying if I said there was no fear going in. Prepped hard the days before — went through the slide deck multiple times, ran through the KNIME exercise files two or three times. The audience was engaged, interested, and the session landed well.

This is worth marking: I was afraid, I prepared properly, and it was a good performance. That's the formula. Fear plus prep equals delivery. Filing that.

## Team OPTIMUM Sponsor Event at adidas HQ

After a longer lunch break and wrapping up a few tasks, Sabine and I drove to Nuremberg to pick up a rental car I needed for the next day, packed the gear, and headed to Herzogenaurach — about 50 km from home — to the global headquarters of adidas.

**The venue itself is something.** You feel the fashion and sports spirit at every step. Our sports club, Team OPTIMUM (the triathlon department of a local club), organized a network event for existing and potential new sponsors. As head of support — basically the master cheerleader — I set up the music box, beach flag, and everything else.

### The Discussion Round — Sabine's Moment

Tim, our head coach and team lead, invited Sabine to a discussion round alongside Isi (Lisa), who is currently the **world champion in double ultra triathlon** — 7.6 km swimming, 360 km cycling, and two marathons, all back to back. And then there was Sabine, who completed her first Olympic distance in roughly four hours last year.

That contrast is the whole point of our team. Not a spectrum of "better to worse" — a spectrum of "everyone is welcome here." World champion and first-timer, same stage, same respect.

I was genuinely proud of Sabine. She talked freely, had great insights to share. And when the moderator asked "how did you start?" she said: *"Well, Philipp, my husband, started with triathlon and I saw it and thought — I'm going to give it a try as well."* That hit me. Proud husband moment, full stop.

### The Two Influencers — A Study in Authenticity

The final session featured two female influencers. Both young, both beautiful women — but in completely different niches. Vivien is in beauty and lifestyle. Anna is a local celebrity who translates Franconian dialect into her reels.

The difference was striking. Anna was fresh, natural, laughing constantly — radiating something real. Vivien was polished. Every surface perfect.

And then the confirmation came when we left the building. Vivien and her friend were standing in front of us, gossiping about Anna: *"Oh she was so cute, maybe we could put the blame on her, everyone will forgive this cute little girl"* — talking about jokingly breaking through the adidas security barrier.

And that sealed it. Everything about Vivien was constructed. Fake hair color, fake lashes, fake nails, fake words. Anna came to Sabine afterwards congratulating her on completing a triathlon with two kids and a full-time job. Vivien and her friend ignored us completely. Authenticity versus performance, live and in person. Fascinating to watch from the sideline.
