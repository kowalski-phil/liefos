# Monday, April 6, 2026 — Easter Monday / Paula's 12th Birthday

## What Happened

**Paula's day.** Today was all about Paula turning twelve. Started with a family breakfast — her birthday, her moment. She loved her presents, and the whole morning had that warm, unhurried birthday energy where the birthday kid just glows.

**Family breakfast — the brother-in-law trigger.** The extended family came together, and it was genuinely nice. But the familiar pattern showed up again: my brother-in-law doing his thing. Talking confidently about AI and data as if he's in the arena, when he's barely in the parking lot. It touches my core competencies — the things I've spent years building real depth in — and he just throws out generic surface-level stuff. I know I shouldn't let it get to me. The metaphor from my Claude desktop conversations holds: the rock in the sea doesn't need to care about every grain of sand that floats by. I like that image. But sometimes the grain of sand still lands in your eye. The kids comparison thing adds another layer — their kids always presented as these little geniuses, which inevitably makes you feel like your own are somehow behind. They're not. But the trigger is real, even if the logic behind it isn't.

**Nurnberger Volksfest — for Paula.** After lunch, we headed to the Volksfest because that's what Paula wanted. Not my crowd, if I'm honest. The atmosphere is... let's say it's a reliable reminder of why I love our quiet village. But none of that mattered because Paula was beaming. We went through it twice, she picked what she wanted to do, and the whole afternoon was hers.

**The bedtime moment.** When Paula went to bed, she said: "Oh, thank you, Daddy. That was a wonderful day." That one sentence made everything worth it — the Volksfest, the family dynamics, all of it. When your twelve-year-old says that unprompted, you know the day landed exactly right.

**TT bike — good news.** Checked the electronic gear shifter on my TT bike yesterday evening, expecting it to be broken. It works. No repair shop, no extra cost. And with the weather turning, a ride is coming soon. The body is ready for more after Saturday's track session.
