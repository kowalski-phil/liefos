# Thursday, April 23, 2026

## What Happened

A genuinely full day — two cities, a training room, a 160 km drive, and a ballroom. By 11 pm I was done. In the best possible way.

## KNIME Level 2 Training — Day Two

Spent the night in Munich before this. Small hotel breakfast, checked out, walked over to Siemens for the second half of the Level 2 training with GBS colleagues.

I was a little nervous going in. I'd restructured things — shifted some content from the previous day into this session, and deliberately applied the feedback that people don't want long theory blocks. Theory, then practice. Theory, then practice. I also skipped the bonus session entirely when I could see people were starting to drift. Better to end clean than to force it.

Overall: successful. Good knowledge shared, the rhythm worked, the session landed.

**What annoyed me:** own team members. Your own people. When I couldn't answer something on the spot, someone dropped: *"Oh, finally we found something Philip doesn't know."* Another person spent ten minutes debating why something was visually incoherent. I thought: if you want to keep doing it the old Excel way, by all means. But these aren't outsiders — they're colleagues I work alongside. The dynamic where your own team is the most critical audience is a real thing, and it still stings a little, even when you know intellectually that it's a pattern and not a verdict.

It passed. The day worked. But I'm noting it because it was there.

**Small disappointment:** my boss and colleagues were in home office. I'd hoped to see them face to face while I was already in Munich. Didn't happen. Just one of those things.

## The Drive Home — and Paula's Welcome

Packed up around 1 pm. 160 km back to Wendelstein.

Walked in the door, and Paula came running. *"Oh, daddy, finally you're back!"* Deep and emotional — the kind of kid-welcome that wipes out a long day in about three seconds. I really loved it.

## OneMIKA

One more meeting before I could close the working day. It went well — cleaner than recent ones. Filed it and moved on.

## Felix's Dance Ball

From 6 pm. This was the real headline of the day.

Felix had been taking dance classes, and this was the final showcase — a proper, formal ball. Suit. Shirt. The works. He looked great. We looked proud.

The program was structured beautifully: a ceremonial walk-in of all the students, then a parent-child dance. Felix danced with Sabine. And then the contest.

Felix and his dance partner Zina placed **second**. 

I watched him out there, and there was a moment — him moving, clearly enjoying it, fully present in it — where I thought: *this is my son, and he enjoys this just like I would.* Not performed enjoyment. Real enjoyment. That's the mirror I didn't expect. That's the thing that stays.

We made great photos. Sabine and I had a brilliant evening. Came home around 11 pm. I was tired. Went straight to bed.
