# Journal — 2026-03-07 (Saturday)

## One Word: Amazed

My son turned 15 today. He invited a bunch of friends over and they basically took over the garden for home camping. We were politely banned from our own backyard — which is completely fine. That's what 15-year-olds do. They don't want adults hovering, and I get it.

The birthday thing hit me in waves though. On one hand, there's the puberty reality — the eye-rolling, the "everything is annoying" phase. On the other hand, I held this kid in my arms what feels like five minutes ago, and now he's closer to choosing a career than to his birth. Time is absurd. But watching him interact with his friends, seeing his values show through — I'm proud. We did something right here. That feeling outweighs any teenage friction by a mile.

On the productive side: I turned an old deck of physical cards into a Claude Code skill. The cards are the **Cicero framework** — a structured approach to public speaking that I bought years ago and never really used. My speeches always felt "good enough," so the cards sat there collecting dust. Today I digitized the whole thing and built a skill that takes my newsletter broadcast messages and uses Cicero as a guide for structuring accompanying videos. Especially useful for conceptual content that isn't hands-on.

But the real mind-blower was NotebookLM. I uploaded the entire card deck content and it generated a 21-minute AI podcast episode — two hosts explaining the framework and walking through a speech topic as an example. It was like listening to a real podcast. I learned more about Cicero in those 21 minutes than in the years I've owned the cards. That was genuinely impressive.

Watched some regular TV in the evening and it just reinforced what I've been feeling for a while: traditional broadcast TV is becoming obsolete. The quality is terrible compared to what I can get from streaming, YouTube, or honestly just a conversation with AI. I have more interesting exchanges with Claude than anything ARD or ZDF could offer me right now.
