# Sunday, April 26, 2026

## What Happened

**Panorama Lauf. Club pride. TT bike comeback. A Sunday that earned a 9.**

## Panorama Lauf — Race Day Support

The Panorama Lauf is one of the signature races in our village — organized by a neighboring club but practically a home event. I'd agreed to get free tickets for some of our starters, and when race day came, we absolutely dominated the field. Our colors, our jerseys, everywhere.

As head of support, I was there from 9:30. Set up the music, the microphone, everything — right at our club home where the race passes by. Beautiful sunny Sunday, loads of supporters out. During the main race we cheered everyone on, and the atmosphere was electric.

Results: our athletes took all three podium spots in the village championship — men's and women's. Plus additional age group places. Complete domination.

## Why I Love This

Two things, really.

First: when else in normal life do you get to shout that loud? "Come on! You got this! You're a fighter!" With music blasting, people running past — there's nothing like it. The permission to be that loud, that enthusiastic, is rare.

Second: I know how it feels as an athlete. Someone on the track cheering you on, giving you a high five, doing the rocky fist — it can change the last kilometer. I do it for our athletes and for everyone else. No filter. Seeing those faces light up, seeing people react to the support — that's outright awesome.

## The TT Bike Comeback

Came home pumped from watching all these performances, and the thought just appeared: maybe I'll ride too.

Put on the cycling kit. Took out the TT bike. First time since Challenge Roth last July.

I was hesitant. Gained a lot of weight since Roth. Wasn't sure if I'd still handle this carbon aero machine with the cockpit and electronic shifters — these are race bikes, and they demand respect. The first few minutes I was unsure. But it all came back.

30 km. 150W average. Nearly 24 km/h. 300m elevation. Aero position held — tedious at times, but it held. One near-fall reminded me to stay careful, and I was for the rest of the ride.

The feeling riding back home: pure satisfaction. The bike still works. I still work on it.
