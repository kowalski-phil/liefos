# Sunday, May 3, 2026

## What Happened

Day three — the ride home. Nearly 150 km already in our legs from the past two days. The question wasn't whether we could do it. The question was how much it would hurt.

## The Route Back

Good news: the navigation found a way around the brutal climb from Friday. What had been a long descent on the way out would have been a long ascent going back — but the map routed us differently, saving about a kilometer and avoiding the worst of it.

We'd analyzed the elevation profile on Saturday evening already. The hardest climb was at kilometer 10 — early enough that our legs were still somewhat fresh. Better at km 150 of the trip than at km 200. We pushed through it without stepping off the bikes. No walking, just grinding.

After that first climb, the rest was mostly descents and minor hills. Around km 15-20 we reconnected with the original Friday track — and flew down that long slope at 45 km/h. The same hill that had us fighting on day one was now pure reward.

## The Landgasthof — Again

Stopped at the same village as Friday. The waiter recognized us: "Ah, you're on your way back!" We said yes — taking everything home now. Full circle.

## Felix's Memory

He would have found the way back without navigation. "Here's where those three guys were repairing their cars on Friday." "Here's where you told me you don't know the region anymore." Every turn, every landmark — he remembered it all. A 15-year-old brain recording the world in real time.

## The Age Gap Shows

Second half of the ride: Felix was literally riding circles around me. Still had power to spare while I was grinding just to keep straight. I gave him freedom — go ahead, we'll meet after a few hundred meters. The difference between 15 and 51, written in pedal strokes.

## Coming Home

Started at 9:30, arrived home at 1:30 p.m. 201 km total over three days. Done.

The welcome was... okay. I was hoping for more emotion from Sabine — some visible pride or excitement about what we'd pulled off together. She was proud of Felix, which was good. But for me, once again, that emotional mirror wasn't quite there. A familiar small disappointment that I notice, register, and move past.

## The Couch Incident

Fell asleep on the couch — natural, earned exhaustion after 200 km in three days. Paula and Sabine found it amusing. "Look, he's sleeping again." Paula even tried to shock me awake. That pissed me off. It always sounds like falling asleep on the couch is something to be mocked, something wrong. I snapped back: "Next time you fall asleep on the sofa, I'll shock you too. See how that feels."

Small moment. Brief annoyance. Not enough to overshadow what we achieved — but enough to notice the pattern.

## Mood & Energy

- **Energy:** 5/10 — completely emptied. Three days, 201 km. The couch claimed me and rightfully so.
- **Mood:** Proud (201 km, all three days completed), fulfilled (the bonding with Felix was real and lasting), slightly annoyed (the couch mocking), exhausted (body said stop).
- **Hydration:** 7/10 — adequate on the ride, less disciplined once home.
- **Standing:** 8/10 — active all morning, horizontal all afternoon. Earned.
- **Exercised:** Yes.
- **Training:** ~55 km cycling. The final leg. Total bike tour: 201 km over three days.

## Key Takeaway

201 km. Three days. No quitting, no shortcuts, no rescue car. Felix remembered every turn, rode circles around me by the end, and neither of us stepped off our bikes on a single climb. The connection we built is worth more than the kilometers — but the kilometers are the proof that we both showed up for it.
