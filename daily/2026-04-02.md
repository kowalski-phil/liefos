# Thursday, April 2, 2026

## What Happened

**Short day, by design.** Started at 10, finished at 4:30. Too many overhours on the Siemens clock, and the long Easter weekend starts tomorrow. No guilt — this is smart time management.

**OneMIKA — the strategy flip.** Two hearts beating in my chest on this one. The customer suggested reducing scope to just 60-70 projects instead of extracting everything. That changes the entire dynamic: no more Power BI errors, no more iterating through columns that never work, no more hitting thresholds that have been the nightmare of this project for weeks. I can finally see the finish line. The data nerd in me whispers "but we could extract everything properly" — and yes, technically we could. But my role here is orchestrator, not driving school instructor. The customer proposed this themselves. That's how we do it. Operator mode: consult with expertise, follow the client's lead when they make a smart call.

**Clean inbox before the long weekend.** This is a behavior I've built over the past few months, and it paid off again. Previously, I'd go into holidays with one or two unfinished tasks nagging at me, draining energy even during rest. Not anymore. Everything that needed doing is done. Clean slate into Easter.

**KNIME slides — sent up the chain.** Ruwen, my boss, told me to send the strategic slides directly to Aram — his boss's boss. "You made them, you should get the laurels." That's excellent leadership. Clean pride sending them — I know what I can do, I'm a pro. Aram and I already had a confident, positive exchange when I applied for the new role back in autumn. Now he sees the strategic work too. Visibility rising, and Ruwen is actively creating the room for it.

**Gründonnerstag — into the Easter cadence.** Went to Maundy Thursday Mass with my wife. The foot washing, as tradition demands. Saw our priest for the first time since his cancer treatment — shook his hand, asked how he feels. Good to see him back.

After Mass, the KAB (Katholische Arbeitnehmerbewegung — the Catholic workers' movement) organized a short 30-minute gathering. This is the part of the Easter story that hits me most personally: the Garden of Gethsemane. Jesus stays with his friends, says "wake with me, pray with me." They fall asleep. And he already knows what's coming. The fact that the Son of God felt fear — because he knew what pain awaited him — is so deeply human to me. That little gathering is our way of saying: all year, you're there for us. Let us be there for you, just this once. Sitting there in that quiet moment — that's church at its most meaningful.

**Choir practice continues.** Ran through the Mass songs again for Sunday. Found out the scanning tool made a few note errors, but correcting them would take too long. In practice it doesn't matter — I know how it actually sounds. Will keep practicing daily until Sunday.

**2 km run — small but present.** Just a short one, but the body moved. Training restart is real. And the bigger win: organized three starting spots in regional races for Team Optimum. The Panorama Lauf deal was especially nice — offered to support with music and cheering, asked for three free starts, they gave us five. Good connections being built with the local running clubs. This was one of those things I'd been procrastinating on, and clearing it felt like lifting a weight.
