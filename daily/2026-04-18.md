# Saturday, April 18, 2026

## What Happened

**Intense, full, fantastic day.** Didn't sleep in because Saturday track training runs until May — picked it up as an anchor and it paid back on both sides: the training itself, and the fact that it sat right in front of the first big support event of the year.

## Kenya Intervals — The Pushthrough

Track morning. Trainer calls it *Kenya training*: run one minute fast, one minute slow, ten times, no watch — you don't get to check yourself, you just go.

The first two or three reps were hard. I heard myself thinking *"okay, I'll stop after the third."* I'm still so untrained right now — nine months barely running since Roth, and the body reminds you.

And then something flipped. I said **no, I'm not stopping, I push it through.** And I did the ten.

That's the win I want to mark. Not the time, not the pace — the pushthrough itself. The muscle of *refusing to fold* is what's actually getting rebuilt right now, more than the legs. The legs will come back. The "don't fold" muscle is what decides whether they get the chance.

## Hilpoltstein Half Marathon — First Big Support of the Year

This one I'd been building toward. **First main event of the season** as head of support for Team Optimum, with more coming up very soon.

**What I did:**
- Picked up the poster at the athlete bib-pickup booth — we're allowed to promote our own October duathlon there as part of the deal with the organizer
- Organized four helpers plus myself for our support zone
- Packed everything: the huge Teufel speaker, microphone, beach flags, tripods, chalk spray, bells, horns, playlist — the full kit the sports team gave me as head of support, which lives at my place now
- Picked up Felix's friends (twin girls) on the way — they came along as supporters

**Personal connection to this race.** Last year I ran it myself as part of the classic Challenge Roth prep: bike the 90 km Roth course, then run the half marathon. I've now run it twice. So standing on the sideline of an event I know by body memory hits differently.

### The Stimmungsnest — 3-4 Hours, Voice Gone

We set up on a hill before a corner — perfect vantage. You can see athletes coming from far off, so you can build the noise before they arrive. Loud music. Microphone. Chalk spray on the asphalt. I switched off on the mic with Flo (our running trainer) so we could each catch a break.

Hundreds of athletes came through. About ten of them were ours — and of course we'd shift to a whole other level of loudness when one of our own came around the corner. The team WhatsApp lit up afterwards: *"support was outstanding once again."* We have a reputation in the area as the club that makes the best party on course. Yesterday we confirmed it.

**"I'm smiling only for you."** A beautiful female athlete came up the hill looking cooked. I shouted: *"Come on, smile, smile — you need to be smiling when you run this hill."* She looked at me with this special look in her eyes and said *"I'm smiling only for you."* That's the icing on the cake. That's why I love this role. **The feedback is immediate and sometimes personal.** You give energy, you see it land, and every so often it comes back with interest.

My voice was sore by the end of the afternoon. Fair trade.

## Felix — The Twins, The Foot, The Bigger Thread

Driving to the event with Felix and the twin sisters. Listening to them in the back of the car, I wondered for a moment whether he might be in love with one of them — the tall blonde is exactly what I'd guess is his type, and they see each other every day at school. But then I listened more carefully: they're talking about handy games. No romantic vibe. Close friends, nothing more — at least for now.

And underneath that thought is something I want to be honest about. **I had a very hard time as a teenager finding a girlfriend.** Everyone around me had one. I was the one who hadn't even kissed a girl for a long time. It was, at certain points, really devastating. And I simply don't want Felix to experience that. If he ends up with one of these girls — great. If it's a boy he loves, also completely fine with me. I just want him *lucky* in a way I wasn't, for a while.

Looking at him realistically: he has good friends, he's well-liked, he spends a lot of time with girls and boys in a natural, unbothered way. He's different from how I was at his age. I think he'll be okay.

### The "Asshole" Moment

On the way home Felix stepped heavily on my foot on purpose. It really hurt. I shouted *"hey, you asshole!"*

Afterwards I was thinking: **there's a better way to handle that.** *"That's not okay, that really hurt"* would have been the right move. I didn't land it yesterday. But naming it here is the first step — **recognition is step one in doing it differently next time.** I'm not going to punish myself for the slip. I'm going to notice it and be ready for the next rep.

## Closing the Day — Greek Dinner

Felix went to the twins' place for a barbecue sleepover with their parents — pre-arranged. So Iris, Paula and I went to a Greek restaurant and had fantastic food. Probably ate a bit too much. Came home totally fed up, watched half an hour of TV, went to bed.

**Wrote a tiny journal entry even though I was wrecked.** Didn't want to break the streak. That's the move — when the bar is "don't miss a day," then even a sentence counts. Today's entry (written Sunday morning for Saturday) is the full version.

Both wins come from the same source — *showing up loudly, on purpose.* For myself on the track. For the athletes on the hill. Cheering is the word. It's what the day was.
