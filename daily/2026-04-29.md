## Presence — The Deeper Thread

Something I've been noticing for months now, and yesterday brought it back.

I looked good yesterday. Cool sneakers, good shirt, nice jacket. I liked my style. And I noticed a reaction — a young woman in the choir (16, a girl really) gave me three or four looks that said "that guy looks interesting." Previously she'd barely noticed me.

I want to be very clear: this is not about her specifically. She's a child. I'm not interested in girls. Period. But the observation is valid because it matches a pattern I've been seeing with women across different contexts — in Munich with colleagues, in everyday situations. People seem to react to me differently. They're seeing something that wasn't visible before, or that I wasn't projecting before.

I think this connects to what started in January with Fenja. Not the specifics of that situation, but what it unlocked: the recognition that I'm still alive in that way. That I'm someone who can be noticed, who has presence. The journaling, the self-reflection, the emotional honesty of the past months — I believe it's changed something in how I carry myself. Not consciously. Not a different walk or a different voice. More like an aura. A presence that comes from being more self-aware, more settled in who I am.

And underneath all of that — the raw, honest part — is what I'm missing at home. Closeness. Depth. Kissing. I genuinely don't remember the last time Sabine and I kissed. Not a peck. A real kiss. Years, probably. I want it so desperately that when the possibility of that kind of connection appears elsewhere, even just as a signal, it changes something in me.

I'm not acting on any of this. I'm not pretending it doesn't exist either. It's here, and it's real.

## The Conversation I Haven't Had

This came out during the journal reflection and I need to put it here because it's the truest thing I said all day.

I haven't talked to Sabine about what I'm missing. Not about the closeness, not about the kissing, not about any of it. And I'm scared. Genuinely scared.

She comes from a family where her father left when she was 12 — left for another woman. That history is always in the room, even when we don't name it. So bringing up "I need more closeness" carries a weight that it wouldn't in another relationship. It can sound like a threat even when it isn't one.

And then there's January. Fenya. If she hadn't pulled back, I would have crossed the line. I know this about myself now. And that forces a harder question: if I was emotionally ready to go there with someone else, what does that say about what's left between Sabine and me? I don't do surface-level — I only invest emotionally. If I was investing in Fenya, some part of me had already withdrawn from home. That's painful to write.

Two fears pulling in opposite directions: if I say something, I might trigger her deepest wound and lose her. If I say nothing, I lose myself — because 30 or 40 more years without closeness is not something I can accept. Both are real.

But there's also light. Maybe she says: "Thank you for saying this, because I'm missing it too." Maybe we're both sitting in the same fear, both too scared to go first. That's not guaranteed. But it's possible.

My instinct for a way forward: talk between the two of us first. No therapist, no third party — just honesty between partners. And then see where it goes from there. I know a professional would help eventually. But the first conversation needs to be ours.

I'm not solving this today. But I'm not hiding from it either. Not anymore.
