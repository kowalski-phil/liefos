# Friday, May 1, 2026

## What Happened

Day one of the bike tour with Felix. 55 km, 150 meters of climbing, gorgeous weather — 25°C, bank holiday, the roads mostly ours.

## The Ride

Started at 11:30 after a generous breakfast and unhurried morning. No reason to rush — hotel check-in wasn't until 3 p.m. Felix on his mountain bike, me on my gravel bike. Bags packed, navigation set, off we went.

First break after about 20 km — power bars and water. Then we spotted a small village just off our route. Our nav would have taken us around it, but it was lunchtime, so we detoured a few hundred meters in. Found a Landgasthof, busy with hikers — a big group of younger men and women doing some kind of May Day hike. Good food, nice hour-long break. The waiter noticed our kit and navigation, asked where we were headed. Small moments like that make the road feel alive.

A few kilometers after lunch: the climb. Two or three kilometers of proper fighting uphill. Both of us sweating, grinding, but we made it. Proud moment at the top.

Reached the hotel at 3:30 — perfect timing. Settled in, then realized we had no shower gel or shampoo. Rode another 5 km to the nearest town, found a gas station open on the bank holiday, bought soap and snacks. Treated ourselves to ice cream in the sun before heading back. Well-earned.

## The Evening

Showered, relaxed, then dinner. Both of us ordered steak. Felix hesitated — old pattern of gravitating toward the most expensive item on the menu, then feeling unsure about it. I told him: "You did the work. Order what you want. Just don't pick the biggest one only because it's the most expensive — pick what you can actually eat." He got it. That became the tone for the whole trip: don't ask, just order what you want. We're here together, quality time, no negotiations needed.

## The Bigger Picture

Good conversations. Good vibe. We were fighting through the kilometers together — at times annoying, at times hard — but always together. Felix is 15, full puberty mode. This trip isn't just cycling. It's bonding time that doesn't come easily at this age, and both of us showed up for it.

## Mood & Energy

- **Energy:** 8/10 — physically tired but emotionally charged. The body worked, the spirit filled up.
- **Mood:** Proud (we made it, including that climb), connected (real conversations, shared struggle), fulfilled (this is what quality time looks like), tired (55 km earns that).
- **Hydration:** 7/10 — drank at stops but could have been more consistent on the bike.
- **Standing:** 10/10 — on my feet or pedaling all day.
- **Exercised:** Yes.
- **Training:** 55 km cycling, ~150 m elevation. Not a race — a journey.

## Key Takeaway

"Don't ask, order what you want." That's the line for the whole weekend. Not just about food — about showing Felix that effort earns freedom, and that I trust him to make his own choices. At 15, that's the gift.
