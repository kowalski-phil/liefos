# Journal — 2026-03-11 (Wednesday)

## One Word: Performing

Up at 4:45 a.m. to drive from Nuremberg to Munich for a KNIME Level 1 training with my own GBS colleagues. Delivered the full day and it went well — but the resistance caught me off guard again.

Some colleagues made snide side comments. "Why does the Sorter Node have such a strange name?" "Can't explain the difference between count and unique count." That kind of thing. I was tempted to snap back — "If you'd rather do this manually in Excel for all eternity, be my guest. I'm trying to show you a way to do less of the boring stuff and more of the strategic work." But I held it in.

What I think is happening: for some people, their Excel knowledge is their secret superpower. When someone comes along and says "here's something that makes Excel even greater," they don't hear the compliment — they hear a threat. Their identity is tied to being the Excel person. I get that intellectually. But emotionally, after years of doing this, it still stings. Especially from my own colleagues.

The bright side: the younger colleagues and students were genuinely engaged and interested. That's where the energy was. Maybe the lesson here isn't about convincing the resistant ones — it's about finding and fueling the curious ones.

Now I'm sitting on a hotel bed in Munich, a bit drained, watching some TV and using AI to make the evening more interesting. Energy was an 8 while performing — now it's dropping fast as the exhaustion sets in.

There's another layer to today that I want to be honest about. There was a colleague I was once drawn to — mutual, clear signals, real chemistry. I was secretly hoping to see her today. I didn't. I know it's better this way. I'm married, she has a boyfriend, and it was simply the wrong time. The logical mind says "good as it is." But there's still a small sting of "what could have been." All theatre of the mind, nothing real. But it added an emotional undercurrent to an already pressured day.
