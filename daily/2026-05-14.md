# Thursday, May 14, 2026 — Father's Day

## What Happened

Father's Day. Not the kind with cards and forced gestures — the kind where a friend drives an hour just to be together.

## Morning — Paula's Offer

Wednesday evening, Paula said she wanted to bake cupcakes for me. I told her: "Darling, this is Father's Day. Don't start baking at 8 PM. And honestly, I don't need a present. What I'd love is for you and your brother to argue a little less — if that's something I could wish for." We had a nice breakfast together Thursday morning, then tidied up the house before the guests arrived.

## Stefan and the Grenadier

Stefan, Julia, and their younger son Tony arrived around lunchtime. Their elder daughter stayed home — puberty mood, and they made the call not to force it. I have a kid in puberty. I know exactly what that looks like.

The first thing Stefan wanted to do: swap cars. He's got an Ineos Grenadier now — the car we've all been making fun of for years. Land Rover Discovery vibes, winch on the front, off-road everything. Completely illogical, completely him. He said, "You drive mine, then I'll drive yours." So I took the Grenadier out, and then he drove our Chinese car.

Two very different machines — his is pure off-road excess, mine is a family van. His verdict on ours: not a Chinese car enthusiast by nature, but for the value you get from the investment, it's a solid deal. Fair assessment.

Stefan is probably a millionaire — inherited shares in the family business, an industrial color manufacturer (pigments for UPS trucks, TV panels, that kind of thing), maybe 50-60 employees. But he shows up in a ridiculous off-road car, hugs you at the door, and spends fifteen minutes in your basement being vulnerable about his marriage. The money is irrelevant. The man is what matters.

## The Basement Conversation

This was what I'd been hoping for all day. I didn't force it — it took time, but before dinner, Stefan and I were alone in the basement, throwing darts, and the real conversation started.

I told him about my situation. The desperation for kisses. The distance I feel from Sabine. He was emotional — genuinely moved, not just listening politely. He completely understood.

His response: I have the right to have that wish. Of course I can't force it — if the other person doesn't want it, that's their answer. But wanting intimacy, needing it — that's not unreasonable.

Then he shared his own parallel: he hasn't slept with Julia in a year. Different need, same loneliness. Different people, different priorities — but the ache underneath is the same.

About Fenya, he was clear: it's good nothing happened. He said even if no one ever finds out, you carry it. He knows because he went through it himself — early in his relationship with Julia, maybe 15-17 years ago, he betrayed her. He still carries the scar. His word: a scar you can't get rid of.

We talked honestly about it. Of course it's charming when a beautiful, clever, younger woman shows interest. Fenya is beautiful and smart. But she pulled the plug, and that was probably the right thing.

His advice for Sabine: don't do it out of the blue — that'll feel like an ambush. Don't provoke a situation to create an opening. Don't force a kiss and then use the rejection to start a conversation. Wait. The right moment will come, and I'll feel it. Then address it openly. Friendly. No accusations. Talk about my needs. Ask about hers. See if there's a way forward together.

I've arrived at similar conclusions on my own and in our conversations. But hearing it from a friend who's walked a parallel road — that lands differently.

## The Rest of the Day

Had cake, raclette for dinner (which always leaves a mess), played darts, drank beer, talked into the evening. They left around 8 PM. We cleaned up together, and then I fell asleep in front of the TV. Earned it.

## What It Comes Down To

After the conversation with Stefan, the clarity kept sharpening. I know what I want: to kiss my wife again. Not as a friend — as a man. That's the request. And a real request means she can say no.

I could dominate. I could force. I could play games. I saw that option with Fenya and chose not to take it — not because I couldn't, but because that's not who I am. The same applies to Sabine. I want kisses that are freely given, not extracted. Anything else is worthless.

The terrifying part of equality is that the other person has full agency. She might say she doesn't want that anymore. And then I'd have to sit with what that means. But that honest answer — even the hardest version of it — is worth more than a forced performance.

This won't be one conversation. It'll be several. She'll need time to process. I'm getting closer to starting it. Not rushing. Not avoiding. Just approaching it at the pace it deserves.
