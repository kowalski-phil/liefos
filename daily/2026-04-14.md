# Tuesday, April 14, 2026

## What Happened

**A 10. First time ever on this scale.** I'm putting that at the top because I want to be able to find this day later. Ten out of ten — not a polite rounding up of a 9, an actual *this is what the ceiling feels like*. And the funny part is it wasn't built on a monumental work day. It was built on a short run in the morning and a soccer match in the evening. That's it. Which is its own lesson.

**The streak-opener run.** Started the day with a very small run — maybe 2 km. Deliberately tiny. I'm thinking about starting a running streak and I want to see if the pattern sticks for me. I already know it *can* — I've done streaks in the past, and I've got hard proof one floor down in the journaling streak that's still running 36+ days deep. When a shape fits me, it fits me. Small and daily beats big and sporadic. The 2 km isn't the point. The *showing up* is the point. Same move as the journal — make the bar so low that "skip it today" isn't even a serious option.

**A short work day, on purpose.** Started late, ended early. Normally I'd wince at that line, but yesterday it was the right call — everything was shaped around the evening. No guilt on the shortened day. The important thing at work happened anyway, and the rest could wait.

**Max-Morlock. Germany women vs Austria. 5:1.** This is the day. We went to the stadium in Nuremberg to see the German women's national team play Austria in a World Cup qualifier. I'm not a soccer guy — I'm not a club loyalist, I don't watch Bundesliga weekends, I only really tune in for the big tournaments like Euro or World Cup. So going in I expected a fun evening out. I did not expect what I got.

**25,000 people.** Yes, women's games usually draw smaller crowds than men's. Doesn't matter. 25,000 is 25,000. When the stadium *erupted* after each goal — and there were a lot of goals to erupt for — it was the kind of thing where you feel the noise in your chest. That physical, collective, unmistakable *this is happening* feeling. 5:1 for the women. A blazing, outright scoring party. One of those nights where the result and the atmosphere reinforce each other into something that keeps going well after the final whistle.

And I think this is why the one-word is *Shouting.* That's what the day was. Not strategy, not productivity, not reflection. Shouting. With 25,000 other people. For a national team in a sport I don't normally care about. And it still landed at a 10, because sometimes the thing that tops you out isn't work at all — it's a shared human moment so big it rearranges the day around it.

A 10 day — the first one I've ever recorded on this scale — and it wasn't built on a work triumph. It was built on two simple things: a deliberately tiny streak-opener run in the morning, and a stadium full of 25,000 people shouting together as the German women's team buried Austria 5:1. Sometimes the ceiling isn't found through intensity — it's found through joy, movement, and a shared human roar.
