# Monday, March 23, 2026

## What Happened

An in-between day. The morning started well enough, but then the OneMika project pulled me into frustration territory again. The data sources are a mess — and I know that's not new, but knowing it and not reacting to it are two different things.

Here's the honest part: I'm too emotionally invested in this project. Not in a productive "I care about the outcome" way — more in a "this should be working better and it annoys me that it isn't" way. The thing is, it's not my job to fix how these massive data sources are set up. I can recognize the problems, document them in the project, escalate if needed — but getting angry about things I can't change is neither helpful nor professional.

Nobody sees it. I'm sitting alone in my home office. There's no audience for my frustration. But I can feel it building, and I need to keep it in check. A little emotional engagement is fine — it means I care. Too much starts draining me and clouding my judgment.

The day got better as it went on. But by late afternoon, the tiredness from two late nights at the end of last week caught up with me. Made the smart call: bed at 10:30. No unnecessary scrolling, no "one more thing." My body said sleep, and I listened. Already feeling today that it was the right decision.

## Mood & Energy

- **Energy:** 6/10 — a mix of frustration drain and accumulated tiredness from late nights
- **Mood:** Frustrated (OneMika data mess), self-aware (emotional investment pattern), relieved (early bedtime)

Recognizing that you're too emotionally invested in something you can't control isn't weakness — it's the first step to showing up more professionally and protecting your own energy.
