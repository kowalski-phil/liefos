# Journal — 2026-03-08 (Sunday)

## One Word: Reflective

Quiet Sunday. The only time I left the house was to vote in the local election. I'd originally thought we might go for a walk — the weather was nice — but it just didn't happen. And that's fine.

The morning was productive. I set up the next cycle for my newsletter — four editions, storylines locked in, first email still needs writing. Then I used my new Cicero skill to create a presentation outline for next week's newsletter broadcast, which I'll probably accompany with a video. Good momentum until noon.

My son's birthday guests were still around. And here's the thing that's still sitting with me: two of his friends pooled money to buy him a used iPhone XS as a birthday present. I googled it — it's probably €100-120. But that's not really the point. The point is that teenagers are gifting each other high-tech devices like it's normal. I would never in a million years have suggested to my kid, "Hey, why don't you and your friends chip in for a used iPhone?" When my son told me, I genuinely thought he was joking. It's the materialism that bothers me. Not the price tag specifically — just the normalization of it. I don't have words for it, honestly. Still processing.

The emotional highlight of the day came in the evening. Watched a documentary about two brothers from a triathlon podcast and how they prepared for Challenge Roth. Hit me right in the chest. Same race I did. There were moments with tears in my eyes. That day always touches me on a deep emotional level — probably because of where I come from. Before 2016, I had zero interest in sports. Started running in my early 40s. Roth will always stand as proof of what a human being is capable of when they truly want it.

And the plans are taking shape: get back into training this spring when the weather turns, build fitness through 2026, go strong into winter, and target Challenge Roth again in 2028. I'll work with my old trainer again starting late 2026. I know there's a risk — the first time was so magical that the second could feel like a letdown. I'm aware of that. But I don't want Roth to be a one-time thing. And honestly, I can't imagine doing a different race. Frankfurt, Hamburg — they just don't compare. Roth is home.

## Mood & Energy

- **Energy:** 6-7/10
- **Mood:** Calm, reflective, emotionally moved, slightly unsettled (iPhone thing)
- **Training:** None

## Key Takeaway

Not every day needs to be high-output — a quiet Sunday with a productive morning, honest reflection, and an emotional reminder of what you're capable of is more than enough.
