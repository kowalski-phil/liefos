Just... present.

May is approaching fast, and I want everything professional and ready before the Berlin event. The AI consulting and coaching path is getting more real by the week.

**Family birthday gathering.** The second half of the day belonged to family. My sister-in-law and her 11-year-old son celebrated their birthdays. We were there until half past ten — good food, nice conversations, a proper family evening.

**The brother-in-law thing.** My brother-in-law fancies himself an AI expert. His knowledge is entirely secondhand — "I talked to this expert, they said XYZ" — people nobody can verify, claims nobody can confirm. He sells it like it's gospel, without actually grasping how transformative (and destructive) this technology really is. A year ago, I would have burned energy trying to convince him. Yesterday? I occasionally pushed back when something was clearly wrong, but mostly I leaned back and thought: I'm the one actually making money from this. I'm the one with hands-on experience, a community, a side business. If he wants to play expert at the family table, fine. The knowledge gap speaks for itself, and I don't need to prove it every time. That's growth. Not dramatic, not a revelation — just quietly knowing where I stand and not needing anyone's confirmation.
