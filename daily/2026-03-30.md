## Reflection: The Operator Principle

Had an honest moment about AI and project work. I caught myself thinking "the AI misguided me on OneMIKA" — and then corrected myself. Blaming the machine is an excuse. AI is the generator, I'm the filter. The machine will never stop me and say "wait, are we solving the right problem?" That's my job.

But there's a practical gap: on solo projects, there's no colleague to push back. AI presents every suggestion with the same confidence, whether it's good or terrible. So I'm building two guardrails into my workflow: a periodic devil's advocate pass (Fridays — force AI to argue against the current approach) and a pre-commit challenge for hard-to-reverse decisions ("give me three reasons this won't work").

The discovery process itself was the point: I complained, AI proposed three options, I filtered them through my own judgment, one stuck. That's the right relationship.

## Key Takeaway

The frustration with OneMIKA isn't about any single problem — it's the accumulated weight of a project that keeps not being what it should be. But the bigger insight today: stop blaming the tool and start being a better operator. AI generates, you decide.
