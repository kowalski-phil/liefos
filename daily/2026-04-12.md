# Sunday, April 12, 2026

## What Happened

**A proper slow Sunday.** Slept in. Didn't rush into anything. My wife was already gone when I woke up — she was out doing her long run for half marathon training. I'm genuinely proud of her. Having gone through the Challenge Roth build myself, I *know* how hard it is to just get into your running clothes and out the door on a grey morning, even without an alarm pulling you out of bed. She's a morning runner. I'm not. That's its own kind of discipline.

**And speaking of the weather — that's where my day broke.** I'd promised Felix we'd take the TT bikes out for our standard 30 km loop after breakfast. Rain killed it. Fair enough. Then all day I was circling the thought of going for a run instead… until around 5pm I heard myself say *"okay, no, I'm not going anymore."* That's procrastination at its finest. I'm naming it so it doesn't just slide past.

**SillyTavern — getting a real handle on it.** Kept playing with the NSFW models and the fine-tuning. It's gone from "promising" last Wednesday to "I actually understand how this tool works now." The tuning reps are compounding. Which matters because Friday's negotiation-training idea lives or dies on exactly that skill.

**The discovery of the day — "30 last days".** This was the moment where AI amazed me again. In the early AI-dopters community, Mark runs a weekly AMA and someone dropped a link in the comments to an open-source project called **30 last days**. What it does: take any topic, scrape Reddit, YouTube, Twitter/X, Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest — basically the whole social web — and pull the signal. Instant market research tool.

I looked at the repo and immediately saw the gap: it didn't route through Apify or OpenRouter, which is my standard pattern for everything I build. So I sat down with Claude, we planned the adaptation together, and Claude built it. Results so far are *very* promising. Still need to wire up LinkedIn and X, and I want to build a small frontend this week. I forked under MIT and the plan is to contribute it back to the early AI-dopters community as a proper community contribution.

**The bigger realization underneath all of this.** I've now built a real stack of useful tools — YouTube media extract, the file-intel pipeline, a bunch of other things — and some of them I barely use because I forget they exist. That's a waste, especially for the ones where I already sunk two or three hours of build time. **I need a content/usage calendar for my own tooling.** Something that reminds me *"hey, you built this, here's when to deploy it."* Otherwise I'm just building a graveyard of clever tools.

**OneMIKA — finally did the work I'd pushed to Sunday.** Not a lot to say about the work itself. More interesting is the pattern: I kept pushing it to "next day, next day, next day" until there was no next day left. That's the same procrastination muscle that killed the afternoon run. Worth noticing both in the same entry.

## Key Takeaway

Classic slow Sunday with one real prize: I forked "30 last days," rebuilt it around my OpenRouter/Apify pattern with Claude, and in the process finally saw the meta-problem — I'm accumulating tools faster than I'm deploying them. The fix isn't more tools. It's a calendar that pulls the existing ones back into my workflow.
