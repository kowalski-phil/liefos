# Friday, April 24, 2026

## What Happened

A productive day, a positioning moment, a delegation challenge, and an unexpected invitation — then an evening that started murky and ended with Freddie Mercury.

## Ideation Olympics — The Heckler

Work ran a relay-style ideation event across several countries. Each country received an idea from the previous, enriched it, and then pitched the result to management. Good format. Good energy overall.

Except for one colleague from the Netherlands who played the role of heckler. When a pitch landed, he immediately moved to tear it apart: *"That's fine in theory, but do you know there are systems that change, and your approach is fundamentally wrong?"*

I jumped in. Not after a pause to compose myself — just jumped.

*"What you're saying might be true. But the core of what these colleagues are doing is AI, and the critical part is prompting — and you can do that in a text file. This is system-independent. The system is secondary."*

Defending colleagues I barely know in a multinational forum, in the moment. That felt right. It also felt like something else: another deposit into the AI specialist bank. Not performed positioning — genuine conviction that happened to land publicly. Those are the best kind.

## OneMIKA — The Delegation Rub

Checked in on the Indian colleague who is supporting me on the OneMIKA project. The task she was meant to handle didn't work out. Gave her a clear assignment to investigate an alternative solution by next week.

I notice the friction in myself when I do this. I'm the type who wants to do it himself. When something doesn't land the way I'd do it, the pull is to just take it back. But that's not the right move here — we're paying for this resource and it needs to be used. If she doesn't meet the bar, that's also a leadership moment. Not comfortable, but necessary. Telling someone *"you need to perform better"* is part of the job, team lead or thought leader.

I'm leaving it with her. That's the right call.

## The Smart Infrastructure Invitation

This one came in out of nowhere and is still landing.

Colleagues from the Smart Infrastructure division — a team I don't even know well — reached out asking if I'd be willing to sit for an interview about Claude and Claude Code in a procurement context. Their reason: my procurement background (20 years) combined with my recent AI expertise (the past two to three years).

That combination is rare. I've been slowly building it. And now it's bringing people to me.

It sounded a little like a job offer. I might be wrong about that. But the direction of it — external divisions proactively seeking me out as the person who understands both worlds — that's the signal I want to pay attention to. I'm genuinely curious where this goes.
