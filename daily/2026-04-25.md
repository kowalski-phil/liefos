# Saturday, April 25, 2026

## What Happened

Slept in after a late Friday night with Bohemian Rhapsody. That was exactly what I needed. The day was packed, but the kind of packed that fills you up rather than drains you.

## Track Training

Got up, had a small breakfast, and went to our weekly track session. The workout: 3x400m at a good and ambitious speed with 400m recovery, then 8x200m at a slightly higher speed with 200m recovery. Solid session.

The majority of our team is currently at a cycling training camp in Italy, but still 8 or 9 people showed up. That says something about the community we've built.

Sabine joined me. We have different speeds, obviously, but just going there together is outright cool. I love doing this with her. It's one of those things that doesn't need to be complicated to be good.

## Race Preparation

After a shower, I went to the club home to set up for a race I'm organizing as head of support. Placed the speakers, the speech flags, everything that needs to be ready. The logistics part of this role — it's not glamorous, but it matters. When race day comes, people notice when things work. They don't notice who set them up. That's fine.

## The Jacket

Realized I have a gap in my wardrobe: proper jackets for winter, a very light one for summer, nothing in between. Drove to a nearby city — ten minutes — and immediately found one I liked. Looks great.

Sabine didn't seem to love it. But here's the thing: I need to like it. It's my personal taste. It's not shocking or embarrassing — just not something she would pick. And that's perfectly fine. I bought it.

## Helge Schneider — The Highlight

This was the evening, and this was the headline.

We had tickets for Helge Schneider at the jazz and blues festival here in Wendelstein. This festival has international reputation — serious guests every year — and this year Helge was on the bill. We've seen him twice before and absolutely adore him. His humor, his style, the way he plays with the audience. A no-brainer when we heard about it.

I laughed the whole evening through. Genuinely, properly laughed. And the cherry on top: we walked there. Ten minutes on foot. No parking, no driving, no planning — just out the door and into the concert. That bonus alone made the evening feel effortless.
