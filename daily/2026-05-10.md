# Sunday, May 10, 2026

## What Happened

Woke up around 8 without an alarm, knowing we had the bike ride at 10. Quick breakfast, woke up Felix. First stop: picked up Paula from her friend's overnight stay. This was the relief I needed — after what Paula told me yesterday about her friend's family situation, seeing the warm, friendly home they live in, the nice mother who invited me in, the happy dogs, all the kids laughing — it softened the sadness from the day before. That girl lives in a loving environment despite everything she's been through.

## The Ride — I Still Got This

Met our friend at 10 a.m. for the group ride — Sabine, Felix, and me. Felix was on my old race bike with the aero cockpit (the one I handed down when I got the TT bike). Beautiful weather, 35 km through the countryside.

We crossed paths with an RTF (Radtourenfahrt) — an organized group ride, not a race, where hundreds of cyclists do 100-150 km and need to finish by 4 p.m. Race bikes everywhere, all day long. We left their official track and did our own route.

The climbs were the real test. I was hoping I still had it, but I wasn't sure. And then — I did. I didn't fall back. I pushed through every climb. Yes, short of breath at the top, yes it's exhausting, but the legs are still there. That confirmation felt genuinely good.

And then there's Felix. On those short distances, I simply have no chance against him. He's 5 cm taller, and when he decides to race away, he's gone. Pure pride watching that. No competitiveness — just a father watching his son fly.

## Afternoon

Came back, showered, then spent some time on the multi-agent system. the work was on the Claude Claw system — improving it so it consistently delivers good content. The multi-agent architecture is genuinely growing. Approached things from different angles and it clicked. Satisfying work, even if it took a bit to get there.

We went for ice cream around 3 p.m. and caught the last RTF riders coming through our village — the tail end of the event rolling past.

## Evening

Back home, settled on the terrace in the sun. Just sat there, a bit of sun protection, soaking it in. Ordered pizza together in the evening, watched a Bergretter (mountain rescue) documentary series — the kind of thing we all enjoy as a family. Wrapped up the day around 8:30, did a bit more OneMIKA work before bed.
