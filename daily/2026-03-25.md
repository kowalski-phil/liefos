# Wednesday, March 25, 2026

## What Happened

A difficult day. One of those days where knowing the techniques to manage frustration doesn't mean they actually work. The anger was real, and it stuck around.

**OneMika — Priya and the IT pattern.** Morning discussion with Priya, one of the IT business partners in the project. She's a symptom of a bigger problem: IT went through a massive brain drain a few years back when the CIO cut jobs right when the market was desperate for specialists. The good ones left. What remains are people who forward tasks to others and then push for premature confirmation that topics are solved. No — a topic is solved when I've done thorough testing, not when you need to check a box. She has a habit of making me look a little stupid in discussions, then immediately wanting sign-off. If she were actually an expert, she'd know that's not how it works.

**The KNIME phase-out threat.** This is the big one. My boss forwarded a slide deck from Central IT proposing to phase out KNIME in favor of RapidMiner — which is now Siemens-owned through the Altea acquisition. The proposal says stop onboarding new projects on KNIME, Alteryx, and similar tools. But here's the kicker: they admit there's no feature parity yet. So what's the alternative? Go back to Excel? Do it manually? The logic is insane.

What makes me genuinely angry: the slides contained false statements. The pricing numbers were wrong. The utilization numbers within the enterprise were completely off. This came from the person running the KNIME business — he either has no clue what's happening in his own small department, or he's deliberately lying. Both options are unacceptable.

This is a decision being made without consulting the business, driven by people riding the Altea hype as a career vehicle. Meanwhile, we're the departments earning the money and paying their salaries. IT is a service provider — why do they constantly create new obstacles for us?

**Fighting back.** Reached out to five or six colleagues to build a coalition. So far, only one has responded. Classic corporate behavior — everyone's there when things are easy, few show up when it gets hard. United we stand, divided we fall. If it needs to be, I'll fight this alone.

But here's the strategic angle: I've spent years building the KNIME ecosystem within Siemens. Around 5,000 installations. Multiple CFOs who approved it as a data transformation tool. A 500+ member community. What I'm going to do is activate those mid-to-senior leaders — either through my contacts or through the community — and make it clear: IT is trying to take away a tool that makes you more productive, without providing an alternative. The installed base is my strongest weapon.

**Choir rehearsal — the bright spot.** Easter Sunday is about 10 days away. We ran through the full program for the final dress rehearsal next Wednesday, when we'll perform with instruments for the first time. The Hallelujah is solid. The mass is solid. A few pieces still need some personal practice this week, but overall it's coming together. This was the reset I needed after the workday.
