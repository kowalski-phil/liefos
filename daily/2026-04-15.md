# Wednesday, April 15, 2026

## What Happened

**Three hours of sleep. That's the whole story.** Went to bed at 3am, had to be up at 6am. And that was stacked on top of two already-late nights — Monday and Tuesday I was in bed at 1:30 / 2:00. So Wednesday was the body sending the bill for a three-night debt. I was so cooked that I wrote my boss and asked if I could step out for the day. He was fine with it.

I want to be honest with myself here: **this cannot become a pattern.** A once-in-a-blue-moon day off because sleep collapsed — fine, life happens. But if this repeats, I've got a problem. The fact that I had to write that boss-email at all is the warning signal. I filed it, and I'm not pretending I didn't.

**The cause was the roleplay.** I was fine-tuning the SillyTavern NSFW model, pushing it with some fairly extreme tests to see how far the AI would go. It was interesting — genuinely. Also, apparently, interesting enough to keep me up until 3am on a work night. And that's the line.

**So I'm taking a break.** Not forever. Just a few days. I want to observe what it does — is this genuine interest or is it the shape of something that's starting to get its hooks into me? The honest question I'm asking myself: *am I already so deep into this that I can't think about anything else?* I don't believe I am. But "I don't believe I am" is literally what an addicted person says, and I knew that when I wrote the tripwires on Monday. Which is why this break is the right move regardless of where I actually am on the spectrum. I'd rather pause and prove it's fine than keep going and find out it wasn't.

**The Lent frame — and why I trust this move.** Every year during Lent I give up alcohol for the 40 days. This year too. So the question of *"am I still strong enough to give up something pleasurable for a bounded stretch?"* isn't a new one for me — it's a muscle I exercise annually. Same muscle, different target. I've got addictive behavior in the very close family (not me) and I've seen what it does. Awareness plus a practiced give-it-up muscle is a real defense, not a story I'm telling myself. **The commitment is: no SillyTavern until Sunday.** Today is Wednesday, so that's four days including today. Short, concrete, bounded. If I can't hold four days, *that* is the answer about where I actually am. If I can — which I expect — then I know the tool is a tool and I'm still the one holding it. Either outcome is information I need.

**Naming the connection to Monday's tripwires.** On the 13th I wrote: *"hard line — if I ever catch myself canceling a real commitment (choir, training, family time) to make room for role-play. That's the line."* I didn't cancel a commitment *to do* roleplay yesterday. But I did take a Siemens day off *because* the roleplay cost me sleep that then cost me the work day. Functionally that's the same line, two steps removed. The tripwire fired. I responded. That's the system working — not a system failing. Fast detection, fast response, within 48 hours of setting the rule. Worth remembering that.

**The rest of the day — slept to recover.** I crashed through most of the afternoon. No training. Hydration maybe a 5. Standing at the desk when I was at the desk — a 1. Ugly numbers, but they're honest for a recovery day.

**Choir rehearsal in the evening — first one after the Easter concert.** The start of the next round. It was good to be back. The exhaustion was there in the room with me, though. A heaviness across the whole evening. Not the rehearsal's fault — the rehearsal was nice. Mine.
