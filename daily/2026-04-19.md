## The Sheet Music Rabbit Hole — And What It Cost

Around lunchtime I sat down with an idea: build something like Sibelius Scan that takes photos of sheet music and converts them to MusicXML. Two to three hours later, I had nothing. Image recognition didn't work. Switched to Gemini. Tried Omer and other OMR tools. Tried images, tried PDFs. Nothing.

Project killed. Not shelved — killed. I don't see a path forward with current tools.

Two things came out of it. The obvious one: some things are genuinely not solvable right now, and knowing that is worth two hours. The less comfortable one: I hit a wall, and it shook my confidence a little. I've been moving fast lately, building things that work, and somewhere along the way I started assuming I could build *anything*. Sunday reminded me that's not true yet. Which is actually good data.

**Opus 4.7 — a real disappointment.** I ran part of this on the newest model and it was noticeably worse than 4.6. The same feeling I had when ChatGPT-5 landed and didn't deliver. And the frustration is compounded by the fact that Anthropic makes it difficult for paying subscribers to switch between models freely. I'm spending over a thousand euros a year. The friction there is genuinely embarrassing.
