# Saturday, May 2, 2026

## What Happened

Day two — the big one. 82 km round trip from the hotel, through Gunzenhausen, along the Altmühlradweg, to Weißenburg, back via the Brombachsee. No luggage today since we were staying a second night. Just bikes, snacks, and each other.

## Morning — Swim Trunks Mission

On Friday evening we'd realized we forgot our swim trunks — the hotel has a pool and we thought it'd be nice after the ride. So the first stop was Gunzenhausen again, quick 10-minute detour into a shop, somehow crammed the trunks into our already-packed bags. Spoiler: we never used them.

## The Altmühlradweg — The Grind

35-40 km alongside a train track. Dusty, boring, no variety, headwind the entire time. Navigation saying "next turn in 3 km" and you look up and it's just more of the same straight track. Both of us found it annoying. This was the mental test of the trip.

Felix later told me he'd been thinking Friday evening and Saturday morning at breakfast that he'd probably give up after 20 km. Instead, he pushed through all 82. The opposite of quitting.

## Weißenburg — The Reward

After Treuchtlingen we decided to push a few more km to Weißenburg rather than stopping early. Good call. Found a Greek restaurant in the city center, locked our bikes, had a proper lunch. Almost too much food — I left some because I knew we still had half the ride ahead and didn't want to carry that weight in my stomach.

Topped up hydration at Lidl, and from there the route transformed completely.

## Brombachsee — The Beautiful Half

Back toward the lake, the scenery opened up. Recreation area, packed with people on this 30°C long weekend Saturday, but we were riding through it on two wheels. Alongside the lake, breaks whenever Felix wanted them. "This is not a fight, not a contest — we break when you want." He used that freedom. One final climb — short but brutal — and then we rolled back to the hotel in the afternoon.

82 km on the clock. Proud.

## The Snake

Somewhere on the first half — a small field snake appeared on the track right in front of me. Too close to avoid, I rode over it. Felix did too. He was on pure adrenaline for three solid minutes afterwards, completely shocked. One of those random moments that become the stories you retell.

## Evening — Earned Rest

Back at the hotel (a Reiterhof — an equestrian farm hotel), we showered, collapsed on the beds, watched TV. The swim trunks sat untouched. Felix asked, "Does it matter if we don't go to the pool?" I said, "No way. If you don't want to, I don't want to either. Let's just rest." Permission granted — to ourselves.

Dinner at the hotel restaurant again. Couldn't imagine going anywhere else. Good food, tired bodies, easy conversation. Fell asleep watching TV, just like the night before.

## Mood & Energy

- **Energy:** 7/10 — legs tired, back burning, but spirit high. The body asked for rest and got it.
- **Mood:** Proud (82 km, the longest day), satisfied (pushed through the boring stretch), tired (earned), connected (encouraging each other through the climbs).
- **Hydration:** 8/10 — refilled at Lidl, drank at breaks, we drank enough.
- **Standing:** 10/10 — all day on the bike or on foot.
- **Exercised:** Yes.
- **Training:** 82 km cycling, moderate elevation. The mental challenge was bigger than the physical one today.

## Key Takeaway

Felix thought he'd quit after 20 km. He rode 82. The boring stretch, the headwind, the heat — none of it stopped him. Sometimes the thing you're most afraid of giving up on is the thing that proves you wrong about yourself.
