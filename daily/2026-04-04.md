# Saturday, April 4, 2026 — Easter Saturday

## What Happened

**First track training of the year.** Got up early for the team's first track session of the season — a proper 400m track, 10-12 people there. Brought the big Teable box for music, but it didn't play — probably not charged properly. A year ago that would've annoyed me longer. Today I let it go in seconds. Not worth the energy. The plan: 400m intervals at threshold speed, meaning a pace you could theoretically hold for an hour. I aimed for 5:30/km, which is ambitious after nine months of barely training since Challenge Roth. Eight rounds, one-round jog recovery between each. I hit 5:30 on average. Every single round. After months off, that's a statement. The body remembers more than you think.

**Sauna and a quiet afternoon.** After the track session, did a sauna session and then let the afternoon drift. Some computer time, nothing heavy, nothing urgent. The kind of afternoon where your body is pleasantly tired and your mind just wanders. Exactly right for an Easter Saturday.

**Easter Vigil — the most beautiful mass of the year.** Went with the family around 8 p.m. The service started at 8:30 in complete darkness. Just candles. For 30-45 minutes, we heard the Easter stories in that dim, intimate light. And then — the Gloria. All at once: bells ringing, organ thundering, everyone singing, every light in the church blazing on. The resurrection, made tangible. It brings tears to my eyes every year, and this year was no different. That moment where you feel the godly spark — where the intellectual fades and something purely felt takes over. That's why I keep showing up.

The mass ran until about 11 p.m. Afterward, helped sell roses for one of our community organizations — a tradition on Easter night. Then Agape in the Vereinshaus: food, conversation, the warm glow of a community that just shared something meaningful together. Home around midnight.

## Mood & Energy

- **Energy:** 8/10 — the combination of physical exhaustion from the morning and emotional fullness from the evening hit perfectly
- **Mood:** Proud (8 rounds at 5:30 after months off), satisfied (the quiet afternoon was exactly what was needed), spiritually moved (Gloria moment, candles, the whole arc of the Vigil), connected (family, community, selling roses, Agape)
- **Training:** 8x400m track intervals at threshold pace (5:30/km avg), plus warm-up/cool-down jogs

## Key Takeaway

A day that starts with proving your body still has it and ends with tears of joy in a candlelit church — that's not a day that fits into one category. That's a full life, lived in full range.
