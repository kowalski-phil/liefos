Which — if you actually understand the stack — is the wrong tool for this job. Knowledge graphs are great, but they need constant maintenance, they don't love conflict resolution across unstructured sources, and the effort/value ratio is bad here. ## The Dance Class — The Part I Didn't See Coming

And it got spoiled.

**My wife cannot accept that in classical partner dance, the man leads.** Slow waltz, waltz, disco fox, whatever — the structure is built around one lead and one follow. Like it or not, that's how the form works. She kept trying to lead and then accusing *me* of doing things wrong. I was fed up pretty quickly.

The harder part — the one I want to write down honestly: **if I ever take a dance class again, it probably won't be with her.** That's not a flash of anger; that's a quiet, settled realization. I love dancing. I want to do it again someday. And I now know something about what that "someday" would need to look like to actually be good. Disappointing in a grown-up way. Not dramatic. Just clear.

Naming it here because this journal is where I'm honest with myself, and pretending I didn't see what I saw would be the wrong use of this tool.
