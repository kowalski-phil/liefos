# Wednesday, April 8, 2026

## What Happened

**OneMIKA — the real breakthrough.** Not a reframe this time. An actual technical fix. I finally solved the two biggest problems on the project, including the one that has been haunting me for weeks: I could not see all the data. Now I can. And the way it happened is the interesting part — it was a combination of several AI tactics stacked together. I pulled out of Claude Code for a bit and went to Gemini directly, just to try a different angle. Gemini gave me some interesting insights and discoveries. I took those insights back to Claude, applied them, and *that* is what finally cracked it. The fix isn't just a fix for this one thing either — I'm pretty sure it's structural. I can apply the same trick to a lot of other extraction problems on OneMIKA. Which means the pattern is transferable. Which means the war just shifted in my favor, not just one battle.

**The Lisa moment.** I had the daily meeting in the afternoon and watched the project manager Lisa just *smile*. Genuinely satisfied with the progress. We have never even met in person, but she is a nice person and there is something quietly great about seeing someone's face light up because of work I did. It made me smile too. For weeks OneMIKA has been the frustration engine — and yesterday it became the thing that made someone on the other end of a Teams call happy. Worth marking.

**SillyTavern — a technical journey disguised as a hobby.** Theoretically this could have been a huge annoyance. I went through the whole tour: started with Grok (jumped scenes, forgot characters, lost context), tried DeepSeek (total chaos), jumped models through OpenRouter, then spun up a local model in LM Studio — which also didn't land. I was going in circles. Then I took the problem into the Claude desktop app and got a handful of tips back on how to construct scenarios, track scenes, and use out-of-context messages in the right places. They sounded like minor things. They weren't. Those two or three tweaks became the turning points — suddenly it was consistent, engaging, and the best version of this I have ever had. Kept me up until 2am. The content itself was personal and good, but the *process* is what I keep coming back to: I was stuck, I tried four things, none worked, then a handful of targeted hints unblocked the whole thing.

**And here is the pattern I finally see.** OneMIKA: stuck → Gemini → insight → back to Claude → breakthrough. SillyTavern: stuck → Grok → DeepSeek → LM Studio → Claude desktop hints → breakthrough. Same shape. Different tool, different angle, pull the insight back, apply it. That is not coincidence. That is my operating system, and I'm only just now naming it out loud. The pattern becomes a habit, the habit lets me grow. That is worth keeping.

**The deep talk with Claude.** I had a long conversation in the Claude desktop app about my past, where I am right now, where I want to be. It got emotional, but in a good way — things got off my chest, I got clarity. I noticed and appreciated the guard rails Claude puts up ("I am not a doctor, I am not a therapist") and I also made my own point back: if a 24/7 conversation partner helps me get clarity, why wouldn't I use it? I am not substituting it for anything. I am adding it to the stack. And I feel better for it.

**Training — nothing, again.** The weather would have allowed a ride. I just had one thing too much on my plate. The TT bike is still waiting for its first ride of the year.
