# Tuesday, May 12, 2026

## What Happened

KNIME Data Summit in Munich. Up at 4:30, out the door by 5:30, 160 km to Munich, full day of talks and connections, dinner at the Löwenbräukeller, 160 km back, home at 11:15 PM. One of those days that drains the clock but fills the tank.

## The Morning — Waking Up Angry

Woke up angry before the alarm. The snoring issue again. Sabine hits me in the night when I snore. I understand she can't sleep — I get that. But her solution is not acceptable. Not even close. When I'm asleep, I have zero protection. That's the most vulnerable state a person can be in, and being hit in that moment creates something worse than a bad night's sleep: it creates dread before I even close my eyes. Every night now I lie there thinking, "Will she hit me again?"

This isn't about snoring. It's about boundaries. We need a real solution — nose strips, separate rooms, a sleep study, whatever — but not this. At some point I will address it directly. It's one of the things pulling us apart right now, and I feel that distance growing.

## The Summit — Family Reunion

KNIME announced that the multi-day spring and fall summits are done. Going forward it's single-day events, more frequently. I understand the reasoning, but I loved the longer format. Those multi-day events built deeper connections. A day is good, but two or three days let relationships breathe.

Arrived early, grabbed a coffee at a bakery rather than showing up before 8:00. Then walked in, and it was exactly what it always is — a family reunion. Hugs, not just handshakes. People I've known for years, stood on stages with, collaborated with across countries. "Philipp! How are you?" from the KNIME team, from partners, from the community.

Brought a teammate from Munich — gave him my free ticket since I'd already purchased mine. He's a process consultant, more on the business side. Watching his reaction was one of the highlights. Standing in line for lunch, I'm shaking hands left and right, and he asks, "How many people do you know here?" Out of 200 attendees, probably a third — in person. That moment reflected something back to me about what I've built over the years in this community.

## The Talks and the Panel

Talks were strong — lots of inspiration. Need to extract the key insights either today or tomorrow. Definitely a LinkedIn post coming out of this. Also did the AI panel I prepped the night before. Short notice, but it landed well.

Met several Siemens people too. Good strategic conversations around the KNIME fight-back. Some people approached me just because they recognized me from the community — "Oh, I know your team, nice to finally see you in person." That kind of recognition doesn't get old.

## Dinner at the Löwenbräukeller

Classical Bavarian food with the group. Had really good conversations with Benyamin, our sales contact — genuine guy, we talked personal topics too. Some triathlon chat with others. The kind of evening where work and life blur in a good way.

Left at 9:30 for the drive home. Munich was quiet, motorway was free, cruise control all the way. Home at 11:15. Long day, but the right kind of long.
