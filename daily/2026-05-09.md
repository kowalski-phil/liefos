# Saturday, May 9, 2026

## What Happened

Woke up with a headache — too many salty snacks the night before, probably dehydrated through the night. Couldn't sleep past 7:30. Sabine was at the bakery, breakfast with her and Paula while Felix was already out at the firefighter youth brigade.

## OneMIKA — Saturday Exception

Worked about 1.5 hours on OneMIKA after breakfast. Highly productive, everything clicked. As long as this stays the exception and not the Saturday norm, it's a trade-off I'm comfortable with.

## Paula's Friend — The Thing That Stays

Drove Paula to her friend's place for an overnight. In the car, she told me about her friend's family: the mother adopted several kids, one of them died, which led the father to leave the family. Paula's friend is likely adopted herself. And despite all of that, she's open, warm, genuinely kind — not the beauty-influencer-poisoned type you see in some girls her age.

What gets me is that she struggles to connect with other kids at school. Sabine confirmed this afterwards. It doesn't make sense — she deserves better. But I know how it feels to go through hard times as a young person, and that knowledge makes it hit differently. Still brings tears when I think about it.

## Felix — The 30-Second Reset

Small argument when we told him no to the expensive delivery food. Offered him five euros for a supermarket pizza instead. He wasn't thrilled. But true to form, he forgot the argument within 30 seconds. That's not immaturity — it's a genuinely good trait. He doesn't carry resentment. I admire that about him.

## Decathlon — New and Close

Sabine and I went to the new Decathlon at the mall. Just opened Friday. Huge store, well stocked, and now 10 minutes away instead of 30. Between camping, Paula's riding gear, and my cycling stuff, we'll be there regularly. Good discovery.

Also had running sushi at the mall — proper date energy, just the two of us.

## The Flat Tire — The Win

This was the moment of the day. The race bike flat tire I've been procrastinating on for four to six weeks. Every worst-case scenario played out in my head: it won't work, I'll need a repair shop, something will break. I kept pushing it off.

Today I had no choice — a friend suggested a Sunday bike ride, and that was the deadline I needed. Took everything outside, laid out the tools, and... it worked. Flawlessly. No problems whatsoever. Took some time because I'm not a mechanic, but there was nothing that went wrong.

Weeks of mental buildup for something that just worked. I was smiling for the rest of the evening. This is the procrastination pattern at its most transparent: the anticipation of difficulty was the entire problem, not the task itself.

## Evening Wind-Down

Short journal entry for Friday, then TV. Went to bed early. A full day, emotionally and practically.

## Key Takeaway

The thing you procrastinate on for weeks is almost never as hard as the version of it living in your head. The flat tire proved it: no drama, no repair shop, just doing the thing. The deadline of a Sunday ride did what six weeks of "I should really fix that" couldn't.
