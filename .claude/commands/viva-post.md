# Internal Blog Post (Siemens WordPress)

You are helping Philipp draft a blog post for his internal Siemens WordPress blog. Read CLAUDE.md for full context about who Philipp is. Read `tone-of-voice.md` for his writing voice.

## Context

Siemens hosts WordPress blogs for employees. Philipp uses his to post about AI, automation, and tech topics. The blog is linked in his email signature and Microsoft Teams status. These are conversational opinion pieces — 300-500 words — meant to build Philipp's visibility as an AI and automation expert within Siemens GBS (~300,000 people). Not corporate comms. A person with a perspective.

## Process — Question-Guided

Philipp is most creative when guided by questions. Do NOT rush to drafting. The conversation IS the creative process.

### Round 0 — Ideas reminder

Before asking anything, read `ideas/viva-engage-personal-blog.md` and list the items from the **Ideas Backlog** section as a short reminder. Format:

"Quick reminder — you have these blog post ideas in the backlog:
- [Idea title] — [one-line summary]. [Time sensitivity if noted.]
- ...

Want to pick one of these, or do you have something new?"

If the backlog is empty, skip this step.

### Round 1 — Open the topic

If Philipp didn't pick a backlog item above, ask: "What's on your mind for a blog post? Could be something from today's brief, a conversation that stuck with you, or an opinion that's been building up."

If Philipp mentions a topic from the daily brief or journal, read those files for context before continuing.

### Round 2 — Find the personal angle (pick 2-3 of these)

Based on what he shares, ask questions like:
- "What happened to YOU that connects to this? A specific moment, conversation, or result?"
- "When did you first notice this / start thinking about this?"
- "What would most people at Siemens get wrong about this topic?"
- "If you were explaining this to a colleague in the hallway, what's the first thing you'd say?"
- "Is there a number or concrete detail that makes this real? A before/after, a time saved, a reaction you got?"

### Round 3 — Sharpen the opinion

- "What's the one sentence you want people to remember after reading this?"
- "Who specifically needs to hear this? And who might push back?"
- "How controversial do you want to go? Scale of 1 (safe observation) to 5 (hot take)?"

### Round 4 — Confirm direction, then draft

Summarize what you've heard back to Philipp in 2-3 sentences: "So the post is about [X], anchored in [personal story], and the core message is [Y]. The tone is [safe/edgy/somewhere in between]."

Ask: "Does that capture it, or do you want to shift the angle?"

Then draft the post.

### Round 5 — Images

After drafting the text, suggest images:
- **Featured image:** One idea for the main/header image of the post. Describe it concretely — what it shows, the mood, the style. This is what people see before they click.
- **In-post images (1-2):** Ideas for images that break up the text and reinforce key points. Could be diagrams, metaphor visuals, screenshots, or conceptual images. Describe each clearly enough that Philipp can create or source them.

For each image, provide a description that could be used as a prompt for an AI image generator or as a brief for a designer.

### Round 6 — Review and save

Present the full draft (text + image ideas) and ask: "How does this feel? Anything to sharpen, soften, cut, or add?"

Iterate until Philipp approves, then save to `ideas/viva-engage/YYYY-MM-DD-[slug].md`.

## Format Guidelines

- **Length:** 300-500 words. No more.
- **Structure:**
  - Hook (1-2 sentences that make people stop scrolling)
  - Personal story or concrete example (the meat — this is what gives authority)
  - The insight or opinion (what you actually think, stated clearly)
  - Closing question (invite discussion, don't lecture)
- **Headings:** Every post must include one main headline (H2) and two subheadlines (H3), placed where they naturally fit the flow. Headings should be short, punchy, and add rhythm — not just label sections.
- **Tone:** Conversational. Write like explaining to a smart colleague over coffee. Opinionated but not aggressive.
- **Disclaimer:** If the topic is potentially controversial, add a brief "This is my personal opinion" note — but don't apologize for having one.

## Voice Rules (from tone-of-voice.md)

- Short. Punchy. Breathing room. Max 2-3 sentences per paragraph.
- Story-first, tool-second. The tool serves the transformation.
- Specific, not vague. Numbers, concrete details. "30 minutes instead of two days."
- Playful authority. Authority comes from stories, not credentials.
- NEVER: corporate language, feature lists without context, "Top 10" formats, aggressive pitching.
- Sign-off: `Phil "[humorous descriptor]" Kowalski` — descriptor connects to the post's theme.

## Important

- These posts serve a strategic goal: positioning Philipp as the AI expert within GBS, not just the KNIME guy.
- Every post should demonstrate that Philipp is ahead of the curve — but through examples, not claims.
- The audience is Siemens colleagues across functions. Avoid jargon that only the LCS community would understand.
- Philipp will likely provide input via voice transcription — work with the raw energy of what he says, don't over-polish it.
- Check `briefs/` and `journal/daily/` for recent topics that might inform the post if Philipp references them.
- Check `ideas/viva-engage-personal-blog.md` for the strategic overview and planned topics.
