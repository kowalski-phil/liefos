## Themes & Categories

### Agentic AI: Buzzword vs. Reality
- "54% of CFOs prioritize AI agents" sounds great on a slide — but what does it actually mean in practice?
- Key question: do people even understand what agentic AI is vs. other AI concepts?
- Core definition: an AI that self-decides which "tool" to use
- The real challenge: how do you actually *build* this? In n8n? In KNIME? Both?
- The "black box" fear: if you hand this to an external supplier, you end up with another opaque system you can't audit or understand
- This is especially dangerous in finance, where auditability and transparency are non-negotiable

## Content Opportunities

### 1. "What Agentic AI Actually Means for Finance Teams" (HIGH POTENTIAL)
- **Format:** YouTube video + newsletter companion piece
- **Angle:** Start with the 54% stat. Then ask: "But do CFOs actually know what they're asking for?" Break down agentic AI in plain language, then live-build a simple agent in n8n or KNIME that solves a real finance problem (invoice routing, anomaly flagging, etc.)
- **Why it works:** Bridges the gap between buzzword and reality. Your finance audience is hearing this term from their leadership and has no idea what to do with it. You become the translator.
- **Newsletter angle (Tina-style arc):** Finance team hears "agentic AI" from the CFO → nobody knows what it means → someone builds a black box → it quietly makes bad decisions → the real lesson: build it yourself with low-code so you can see inside.

### 2. "The Black Box Problem: Why You Should Build Your Own AI Agents"
- **Format:** LinkedIn post or newsletter
- **Angle:** The danger of outsourcing AI agent development to vendors who give you zero transparency. In finance, if you can't explain how a decision was made, you have a compliance problem. Low-code tools like n8n and KNIME let you keep control.
- **Why it works:** Speaks directly to the fear finance professionals have. Positions low-code as the responsible choice, not just the cheap one.

### 3. "Your Moat Isn't Your Features" (Brand/Personal)
- **Format:** LinkedIn post
- **Angle:** Riff on the Anthropic story. What you stand for can't be copied. Apply it to anyone building a personal brand or a consulting practice.
- **Why it works:** Thought leadership that's not about tech — shows range, resonates broadly.
