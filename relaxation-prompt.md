Section headers use heavier pencil pressure. The icons are simple, minimal line drawings — not polished, more like quick sketches in a field notebook. [Composition] Portrait orientation, single page, structured like a well-worn pocket reference card. Generous whitespace between sections. The layout breathes — not crowded. Top-down visual hierarchy: title, five techniques with icons, divider, reframe box, closing line.

[Materiality] The paper itself is the star — warm beige-brown kraft paper with visible fiber texture, slightly yellowed at the edges. The coffee stains are translucent amber-brown rings, dried and organic. One corner is slightly dog-eared. The overall feel is a personal cheat sheet that has lived in someone's desk drawer for months and been pulled out dozens of times.

Do not include any photographs, 3D renders, or digital UI elements. Everything should feel analog, tactile, and hand-made.
