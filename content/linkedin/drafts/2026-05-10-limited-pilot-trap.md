# LinkedIn Draft: The Limited Pilot Trap
**Date:** 2026-05-10
**Status:** Draft
**Format:** Text post (value/story)
**Source:** Rory's brief, idea #3
**Topic:** 45% of finance teams stuck in pilot mode -- what the 17% who broke through did differently

---

45% of finance teams are running an AI pilot right now.

Most of them will still be "running the pilot" in 2027.

I've seen this pattern so many times I can predict the ending before it starts.

It goes like this.

Someone goes to a conference. Gets excited. Pitches the CFO on "exploring AI for finance ops."

CFO says yes -- but cautiously. "Start small. Keep it limited. We'll review in Q3."

And then the pilot just... lives there. In a corner. Nobody owns it. Nobody measures it. Q3 comes and goes.

Sound familiar?

Here's what separates the 17% who actually get AI into their core workflows.

They don't start with AI. They start with the most painful process nobody wants to touch.

One process. High volume. Lots of manual steps. Hours nobody can get back every month.

They automate that end-to-end. Not 80%. End to end.

Then they put the time savings in front of the CFO. Not a slide deck. An actual number.

"We got 14 hours a month back. Here's what that costs us per year."

That's it. That's the whole playbook.

The pilot trap isn't a technology problem. It's a scope problem.

Too broad. Too vague. Too many stakeholders with too little ownership.

Pick the one ugly process everyone's embarrassed about. Fix it completely. Then talk to the CFO.

The rest follows.

What's the "pilot in the corner" in your org right now? I bet someone on your team knows exactly which one I mean.
