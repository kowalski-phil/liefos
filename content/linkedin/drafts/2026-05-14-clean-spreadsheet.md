# LinkedIn Draft: The Clean Spreadsheet
**Date:** 2026-05-14
**Status:** Draft
**Format:** Text post (contrarian/value)

**Topic:** AI chatbot request that's really a data cleanup problem

---

My team asked for an AI chatbot.

What they actually needed was a clean spreadsheet.

I see this pattern every week now. Someone walks in with "Can we build an AI thing for this?"

I dig in. And nine times out of ten, the real problem is:

Data in four different formats.
No single source of truth.
Manual copy-paste between three systems that should've been connected years ago.

You don't need AI. You need plumbing. First.

Here's a real one from last month.

A procurement team wanted an AI agent to answer questions about supplier contracts. Smart idea. Genuinely useful.

But their contract data lived in SAP, two SharePoint folders, a shared drive nobody maintained, and -- I wish I was joking -- someone's Outlook inbox.

We could've spent six weeks building the agent. It would've given confident, beautifully formatted wrong answers. Every time.

Instead, we spent three days connecting the data sources. One workflow. One clean output. Took the contract lookup from 45 minutes to about 90 seconds.

No AI required.

The boring part nobody wants to fund is the part that actually works.

Data cleanup. Process mapping. Connecting systems. This isn't the sexy stuff that gets budget approval in the first meeting.

But it's the foundation that makes the sexy stuff actually function.

I've been doing this for 25 years at Siemens. The technology changes every cycle. The pattern doesn't.

People skip the foundation. Build something impressive on top. Watch it wobble. Blame the technology.

The technology was fine. The spreadsheet was a mess.

So here's my unsolicited advice: Before you pitch that AI project to your CFO, ask yourself one question.

If I plugged an AI into our data right now -- would I trust the answers?

If the answer is no, you know where to start. And it's not where you think.

What's the most "un-sexy" fix that actually transformed a process in your team? I bet the story is better than any AI demo.

Phil "plumber first, data scientist never" Kowalski
