Cloudflare just cut 1,100 people.

The CEO's explanation? "Agentic AI changed everything."

I call BS.

Not on the layoffs. Those happened.

But on the framing.

Companies have been sitting on structural waste for years. Roles that existed solely to move data from one place to another. Processes that should have been automated in 2019.

AI didn't create that problem. It just made the excuse available.

Here's the thing -- if your entire job is transferring information from A to B, you were always one automation cycle away from this moment. AI just accelerated the timeline.

But that's not most people.

Accenture just published something that cuts against the panic: 95% of employees at AI-advanced firms report HIGHER job satisfaction.

Not lower. Higher.

Because AI took the copy-paste. The status updates. The weekly pivot table nobody wanted to build.

And left the judgment. The context. The relationships.

I've watched this play out inside a 500-person low-code community at Siemens. The people who automated their most painful workflows didn't lose their jobs.

They became the ones who knew how.

The Cloudflare story is real. And it's also a distraction.

The real question isn't "is AI coming for my job?"

It's: what's the most repetitive thing you do every week -- and what would it take to get it off your plate this month?

Phil "robots took my pivot table, not my job" Kowalski
