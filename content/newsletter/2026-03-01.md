# Week 3 — "My automation lied to me"

**Subject:** Green lights. Wrong numbers.

---

Ever trusted a report just because the worfklow ran without errors?

Tina worked in treasury. Three bank CSVs, one daily cash position report, all automated with KNIME.

Click Execute, green lights, report on the manager's desk.

Ten minutes. Done.

Three weeks in, she decided to clean things up.

Some transaction types didn't belong in the daily report.

So she added a Row Filter node.

"Exclude everything where Transaction_Type does not equal External"

Simple. Logical. Took her two minutes.

The workflow still ran. Green lights. Report still looked right.

But something had changed.

A handful of back entries had no transaction type at all.

The field was just ... blank.

And the Row Filter? It didn't know what to do with the blanks.

So it quietly removed them, too.

Not because they were wrong.

Because they were empty (aka "... not equal External")

Seven rows. Gone.

Every day. For two weeks.

The cash position was off. Not dramatically - just enough that nobody noticed.

.

.

Until someone did.

What the ... ?

The fix wasn't to remove the filter node.

It was to replace it with something smarter.

A Row Splitter node.

Same logic. But instead of one output, you get two.

Top port: clean rows. Business as usual. These flow into the report.

Bottom port: everything the condition caught - including these rows with blanks.

These go to a separate file. A small one.

A "hey, Tina, have a look at these" file.

One node. Two output ports. Zero data lost.

Don't delete what you don't understand. Split it - one output for the report. one for the rows that need a second look.

That little review file made Tina ask a dangerous question.

"What else in this report goes unnoticed???"

She deleted half her workbook after that question.

More on that next Sunday.

Phil "Trust But Verify" Kowalski
