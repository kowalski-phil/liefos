## The AI Isn't Broken. Your Input Is.

I got angry at AI last week.

Not a little frustrated. Genuinely annoyed. I asked it something, and it made stuff up. Confidently. Beautifully formatted nonsense.

My first thought: "This tool is broken."

My second thought — after a coffee and some honesty: "Wait. Did I actually tell it to research this? Or did I just assume it would?"

Spoiler: I assumed.

### The Uncomfortable Truth

Here's what I've learned after months of working with AI daily: **every time I thought the tool was broken, the problem was my input.**

Vague prompt? Vague answer. No context? It fills in the blanks — with confidence. That's not a bug. That's how the technology works.

AI is a prediction machine. It generates the most probable next words based on what you give it. It's not Excel. There's no column A times column B equals column C. It doesn't think in absolutes. It thinks in probabilities.

And that's actually a superpower — if you know how to use it.

### One Word Is Not a Prompt

I've seen it over and over. In SiemensGPT. In KNIME AI. Colleagues type in one word — maybe two — and expect a perfect result. Then they close the tab and tell everyone "AI doesn't work."

It does work. But it needs you to meet it halfway.

Tell it what role to play. Give it context. Be specific about what you want. And if you need facts — tell it to look them up, because it won't do that on its own.

That's not a limitation. That's a skill. And like any skill, it gets better with practice.

The moment I stopped blaming the tool and started improving my input, everything changed. Fewer errors. Better results. Less frustration.

If everything works perfectly on your first try, you're probably not pushing the boundaries. The "errors" are where the learning happens. They show you exactly where your thinking was vague.

**The AI isn't broken. And neither are you.** You're just learning a new language. Give yourself — and the tool — a fair chance.
