# Is Low Code Dead? — Viva Engage Post

**Status:** Draft ready to publish
**Date:** 2026-03-06
**Controversy level:** 4/5
**Key line:** Don't think in absolutes. Think in growth.

---

"Is low-code dead?"

I keep seeing this question. In articles. In comments. In hallway conversations.

Here's my honest answer: it's the wrong question.

We've been asking "Is X dead?" for decades. Books. Radio. Excel. Spoiler: none of them died. They evolved.

I've been working with KNIME and automation tools for years now. Recently, AI entered the picture — and yes, it changes things. But here's what I notice: every time a new technology arrives, people jump to absolutes. "This replaces that." "That tool is dead." "Just use AI for everything."

That's not how it works. Not in real life. Not in enterprises.

Think about it. Excel was probably better than paper. KNIME enhanced Excel with automation and machine learning. AI now enhances KNIME — it helps you build workflows faster, understand data better, find patterns you'd miss.

Each layer amplifies the one below it. It's a growing tech stack, not an exchanging one.

And here's the part that doesn't get enough attention: auditability.

Yes, you can let AI generate a dashboard, a finance report, an entire app for you. Vibe coding, they call it. And if you're working completely alone on something that only you will ever see — maybe that's fine.

But at Siemens, you're rarely working alone. Everything you do is part of a network. Like a spider's web — every movement you make causes vibrations that touch other people. Your colleague needs to understand your workflow. Your auditor needs to trace your logic. Your successor needs to pick up where you left off.

Black boxes don't survive in that environment. Tools like KNIME and n8n make it crystal clear what happens where, step by step. That's not a limitation. That's a feature.

So no, low-code isn't dead. It's evolving. Like everything else before it.

Don't think in absolutes. Think in growth.

*This is my personal opinion, shaped by years of working with these tools across GBS.*

Phil "Growing Stack" Kowalski
