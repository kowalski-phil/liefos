# Vibe Working — What Is It and Why Should You Care?

**Status:** Draft ready to publish
**Date:** 2026-03-06
**Controversy level:** 3/5
**Key line:** AI isn't here to replace you. It's here to check off your "I always wanted to do this" list.

---

Last week, I was in an AI exchange with a colleague. Someone who's also in the AI space. I was talking about how I use AI in my daily work — and I noticed something.

Blank stares.

Not confusion. Not disagreement. Just... a gap. The tools I've been using for months — tools that have fundamentally changed how I work — weren't even on his radar.

That moment got me thinking.

There's a term floating around right now: "vibe working." You might have heard of "vibe coding" — where you describe what you want and AI builds it. Vibe working is the bigger picture. It's when AI stops being a tool you use and becomes a partner you work with.

Here's what that looks like in practice.

Last week, I had a massive Excel workbook. 35 sheets. Over 240,000 formulas. I needed to understand the dependencies — which sheets connect to which, what can safely be changed, where the risks are. Manually? That's a two-day job, minimum. With AI as my working partner, it took 30 minutes. And the result was better than what I would have produced alone.

That's not AI replacing me. That's AI letting me do what I always wanted to do — the interesting work — instead of drowning in the tedious parts.

I keep noticing a pattern. Many of us draw a hard line between our private and professional tech lives. We discover amazing tools at home — and then walk into the office and pretend they don't exist. I did the opposite. I started using n8n privately, fell in love with it, and then discovered it was available at Siemens. Same with Claude Code. The crossover wasn't a risk. It was an accelerator.

Here's what I think most people get wrong: they hear "AI" and think "replacement." But vibe working isn't about doing less. It's about finally getting to the things on your "I always wanted to do this" list.

That analysis you never had time for. That process you always wanted to redesign. That idea you parked because the groundwork was too tedious.

AI doesn't take those away from you. It clears the path to them.

We're living in amazing times. The question is: are you using them?

Phil "Vibing at Work" Kowalski
