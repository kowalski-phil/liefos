# I Owned It for Years. AI Taught It to Me in 21 Minutes.

I bought a public speaking framework a few years ago. Physical cards. Beautiful design. A structured system for building better speeches.

I opened the box once. Flipped through a few cards. Put them back.

My speeches were "good enough." So the cards collected dust.

## The 21-Minute Breakthrough

Fast forward to last weekend. I uploaded the entire content of that card deck into an AI tool. Asked it a few questions. Got solid answers.

But then it did something I didn't expect.

It generated a 21-minute podcast episode. Two AI hosts explaining the framework, walking through a real example, discussing the nuances — like a proper interview you'd hear on Spotify.

I learned more in those 21 minutes than in years of owning the cards.

Not because the information changed. Because the *format* changed.

### The Real Bottleneck Isn't Content

Here's the thing — we all have stuff like this. Books we bought but never read. Courses we enrolled in but never finished. Tools we have access to but never explored.

At Siemens, we have tens of thousands of learning resources on MyLearning World. LinkedIn Learning alone is a library most universities would envy. But having access isn't the same as learning. The bottleneck was never the content. It's finding the right entry point.

AI can be that entry point.

Not by replacing learning. By making it *accessible* in the way that works for you. A podcast instead of a manual. A conversation instead of a course. A summary that tells you where to dig deeper.

### A Door That's Already Open

If you're curious about where to start — our **GBS Low Code Service** knowledge board offers practical learning paths for KNIME, n8n, and RapidMiner. Real tools. Real use cases. Built by colleagues who use them every day.

Sometimes the best learning hack isn't more content. It's a different door into the content you already have.

**What's something you've had sitting around — a course, a book, a tool — that you never got around to learning? What if AI could crack it open for you in 20 minutes?**
