## You Didn't Choose Your AI — Your Phone Did

Yesterday, two things happened within hours of each other.

Microsoft announced that Claude — built by Anthropic — will power the reasoning engine inside Microsoft 365 Copilot. The new feature is called Copilot Cowork. It builds presentations, pulls data into Excel, coordinates across Outlook. All powered by Claude.

The same day, Apple confirmed that the new Siri will run on Google's Gemini model.

Let that sink in for a moment.

### Two Giants, Same Conclusion

Two of the most powerful tech companies on the planet — companies with essentially unlimited engineering resources — independently decided: we're not building our own AI brain. We're partnering with the best one available.

Microsoft went with Claude for enterprise. Apple went with Gemini for consumers.

The strategic logic is identical. Focus on what you're great at — Office ecosystem, iPhone ecosystem — and plug in the best AI for your audience.

Here's the thing.

Most people will never know this happened. They'll just open Word or talk to Siri and assume it's "Microsoft AI" or "Apple AI." They'll never ask which model is running underneath.

And that's the real insight: **most people don't choose their AI. Their phone chooses. Their employer chooses. Their IT department chooses.**

I see this all the time. Someone uses Gemini because they have an Android phone. Someone else uses Copilot because it's in their Office toolbar. Not because they evaluated the options. Because it was *there*.

### So Does It Matter Which Model You Use?

I think it does. Massively.

I work with multiple AI models daily. The differences in reasoning, nuance, and the ability to handle complex tasks are enormous. Gemini is genuinely impressive at image generation — it's a spaceship compared to a stone wheel there. But when it comes to structured thinking, following complex instructions, and working through multi-step problems? That's where models like Claude pull ahead.

The point isn't which model is "best." The point is: **if you don't know what's powering your AI, you can't make an informed decision about what to use it for.**

And in an enterprise like ours, where AI is becoming part of how we work every day, that awareness matters.

### What This Means for Us

The AI landscape is moving fast. The companies building the tools we use are making strategic bets on our behalf. That's not good or bad — it's reality.

But the people who understand what's happening underneath? They'll use these tools differently. Better. More intentionally.

You don't have to become an AI researcher. But knowing which engine is under the hood? That's the difference between driving and being driven.

So — do you know which AI model you used today?

Phil "Reading the Fine Print" Kowalski
