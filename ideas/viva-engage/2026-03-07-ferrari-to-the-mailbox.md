## Post

We have AI. Real AI. Not the sci-fi kind. The kind that analyzes a 35-sheet Excel workbook with 200,000 formulas in 30 minutes. The kind that builds entire workflows from a conversation. The kind that turns a rough idea into a structured project plan before your coffee gets cold.

And what do most of us use it for?

Improving emails.

Don't get me wrong. Better emails are nice. But it's like buying a Ferrari and only driving it to the mailbox.

Here's the thing.

Our CEO says AI is the way forward. Anthropic just published research showing that actual AI adoption is a fraction of what's technically possible. Not at some startup. Everywhere. Including companies like ours.

I see it every day. We have Siemens GPT. We have bots. We have tools. The capability is there. The adoption isn't.

Why?

Three reasons, I think.

**One: Overload.** Everyone's busy. AI is "just another thing on the desk." When you're already drowning in deliverables, the last thing you want is someone telling you to learn something new. I get that.

**Two: Speed of change.** The AI news cycle is relentless. New model every week. New tool every month. It's exhausting just keeping up. So people stop trying.

**Three: Permission.** This is the uncomfortable one. We're a German engineering company at our roots. We like structure. We like approval. We like knowing something works before we touch it. "Play around with it" doesn't come naturally in a culture that values precision.

But here's the kicker.

AI doesn't reward precision-first thinking. It rewards curiosity-first thinking. The people getting the most out of AI right now aren't the ones who read the manual first. They're the ones who opened the tool and started a conversation.

Last week, a colleague came to us with a monster Excel workbook. 35 sheets. Over 200,000 formulas. They needed to understand the structure — which formulas feed into which, where the dependencies are, what could break. Manually? That's days of work. Maybe a week. We sat down with AI and built a complete formula directory in about 30 minutes.

That's not magic. That's what happens when you stop using AI for cosmetics and start throwing real problems at it.

Forget the engineering mindset for a moment. Start playing.

Ask it a stupid question. Give it a messy problem. Let it surprise you. The worst that happens? You lose ten minutes. The best? You find three hours you didn't know you had.

The gap between what AI can do and what we actually use it for — that's not a technology problem. It's a mindset problem.

And mindset? That's something each of us can change. Starting today.

If you want to explore what AI can actually do for your team — not theory, but hands-on — reach out. That's exactly what we do in the Low Code Service. No pitch. Just a conversation.

What's stopping you from going beyond better emails?

*This is my personal perspective, not an official Siemens position.*
