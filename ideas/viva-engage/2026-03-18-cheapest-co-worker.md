## My Cheapest Co-Worker Doesn't Drink Coffee

Last Friday, a colleague asked me: "Do you offer training on AI prompting?"

I didn't have a product for that. Not yet.

By Tuesday afternoon, I had a full curriculum, a confirmed price point from my boss, and a slide deck to sell it.

### Friday to Tuesday. That's It.

Here's what happened. Friday, the question landed. I parked it — didn't rush. On Monday morning, I took the 5-page overview I already had for our self-paced online course and told my AI: "Turn this into a paid in-person workshop."

Seventeen pages came back. A full outline. Not something I'd show a customer — but everything I needed to guide the conversation with my boss.

Tuesday, 1:30 p.m. Meeting with my boss. "What price point are you thinking?" Same number I had in mind. Green light.

Tuesday, 3:00 p.m. Team meeting. I was listening in, not actively participating. So I opened Claude Code and said: "Let's brainstorm how the sales slides should look." It sketched them out in ASCII art — pure text, no fancy plugins, no scripts, no downloads. I looked at the structure, opened PowerPoint, and built the real slides myself.

By 4:00 p.m., I had a sellable product. Curriculum. Pricing. Deck. Done.

### The Trick Nobody Talks About

Most people think "AI-generated slides" means wrestling with complex prompts, installing extensions, downloading scripts. That's overcomplicating it.

I used a command-line AI tool. It can't even create PowerPoint files. What it *can* do is think with me. Brainstorm layouts. Structure arguments. Sketch in text what I then build visually.

That's the hybrid approach. AI handles the thinking. I handle the finishing.

Here's the thing — I could have done all of this without AI. The curriculum, the slides, the research. But it would have taken me a week. Maybe two. And by then, the momentum from that Friday conversation would have been gone.

### The Real Shift

AI is probably the cheapest co-worker you can get. Not because it replaces what you do. But because it compresses the time between "I have an idea" and "I have a product."

And when execution gets that fast, something changes in your head. You stop thinking like someone who executes tasks. You start thinking like someone who makes decisions.

That's not a time-saving story. That's a career story.

*So here's my question: what idea have you been sitting on that could be real by next Tuesday?*

Phil "From Idea to Invoice" Kowalski

*Disclaimer: This is my personal opinion and experience. Your mileage may vary — but I'd bet it's closer to mine than you think.*
