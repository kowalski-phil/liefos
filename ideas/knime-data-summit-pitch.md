## Talk Narrative Arc

The ProcureCon stats tell the whole story in four beats:

**1. "45% want AI in procurement, but only 11% are ready."**
→ The ambition is there. The capability isn't.

**2. "67% cite data privacy and security as the main barrier."**
→ Should we send our core cost management data to public AI interfaces? Probably not. This is where local, on-premise tools like KNIME matter. You keep your data where it belongs.

**3. "54% say insufficient data quality holds them back."**
→ This is where KNIME steps in before AI even enters the picture. Send shitty data to AI, complain that the result is garbage — that's not an AI problem, that's a data preparation problem. KNIME solves this.

**4. "54% say securing digital talent is their top challenge."**
→ You don't need to hire Python developers. You need to take the process experts you already have — the people who know procurement inside out — and give them approachable tools. You don't need Pandas, Matplotlib, or debugging missing brackets. You need to spot patterns, and that skill already exists in your organization. KNIME marries process expertise with digital capability.

**The punchline:** "54% of CPOs can't find digital talent. That's what I do — I take regular people and give them digital superpowers. And we live in a time where the tools have finally caught up."
