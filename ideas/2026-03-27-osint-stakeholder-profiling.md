# OSINT for Stakeholder Profiling

Apply Open Source Intelligence (OSINT) techniques to stakeholder profiling using a dedicated Claude Code agent or skill.

## Source Material

- **"Deep Dive" by Ray Barker** — considered the masterpiece in the OSINT niche. Phil owns a physical copy.
- Techniques from this book could be systematized into automated or semi-automated research workflows.

## Use Cases

1. **KNIME fight at Siemens:** Profile allies and opponents. Who supports keeping KNIME? Who's pushing RapidMiner? What are their motivations, networks, and influence levels?
2. **AI consulting (side business):** Research potential clients, understand their pain points, tailor messaging for maximum impact.
3. **General stakeholder mapping:** For any strategic initiative — identify decision-makers, influencers, skeptics, and champions.

## Implementation Ideas

- Separate Claude Code project (not in LifeOS — data doesn't belong here)
- Dedicated agent or skill that applies OSINT research techniques
- Could integrate with LinkedIn research, company org charts, public data sources
- Output: stakeholder profiles with influence mapping, messaging recommendations

## Open Questions

- Which techniques from "Deep Dive" are most applicable to professional stakeholder profiling?
- What's the right balance between automated research and manual analysis?
- Legal/ethical boundaries — what's acceptable for internal corporate stakeholder analysis?
- Could this become a service offering in the AI consulting practice?

## Connection

- Directly supports the KNIME phase-out counter-strategy
- Feeds into AI consulting practice (client research as a service)
- Builds on Phil's 20 years of procurement negotiation experience (stakeholder reading is already instinctive — this systematizes it)
